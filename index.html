Proposes a deep learning pipeline that screens 10 million chEMBL compounds to identify GLS-1 inhibitors for aggressive cancers including glioblastoma, hepatocarcinoma, and triple-negative breast cancer. Combines PubChem10 and ChemBERTa transformer embeddings with Random Forest and KNN in a hybrid ensemble, validated by AutoDockVina 3D molecular docking. Reduces early drug discovery from 5-6 years of manual screening to a scalable, automated pipeline with best-in-class RMSE of 0.601.

Addresses breast cancer mortality disparities (India 27%, Africa 60% vs. US 7%) by deploying generative AI on existing ultrasound infrastructure. Evaluates GAN, Stable Diffusion, and traditional augmentation across MobileNetV2 and ResNet50 + XGBoost hybrid architectures. Validated by two Indian radiologists, the model achieved 100% accuracy vs. their 95% average, in 5.87 seconds vs. 3 minutes. iOS app deployed for real-time global clinical use.
